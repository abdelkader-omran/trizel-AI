Overview

AUTO DZ ACT is a deterministic, verification-first scientific algorithm designed for epistemic validation. Its purpose is not to construct theories, optimize models, or interpret physical causes, but to determine whether a given scientific claim is experimentally decidable under explicitly stated conditions.

The method enforces a strict separation between:

  • Theoretical output (T) — what a claim or model asserts should be observed
  • Empirical / observational data (E) — what is actually measured or recorded
  • Decision thresholds — explicitly declared tolerances and comparison criteria

The absence of measurement is treated as a first-class scientific outcome.

Epistemic States (Deterministic)

0/0 — Confirmed
Agreement between theory and data within declared thresholds.
D0/DZ — Indeterminate
Data exist but lack sufficient discriminative power under the declared criteria.
DZ — Contradicted
Significant discrepancy under declared criteria.
∅/∞ — Not Testable
Absence of comparable, well-defined, or accessible data.
