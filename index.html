Overview

AUTO DZ ACT is a deterministic, verification-first algorithm for epistemic validation. It evaluates whether a claim is experimentally decidable by comparing theoretical outputs (T) to empirical/observational data (E) under explicitly declared thresholds.

What it does (and does not do)

  • Separates validation from interpretation (strict T vs. E discipline).
  • Does not optimize models or propose physical mechanisms.
  • Does not assign probabilities, confidence scores, or rankings.
  • Focuses on auditable decision rules and reproducible checks.

Four epistemic states

  • 0/0 — Confirmed: agreement within declared thresholds.
  • D0/DZ — Indeterminate: data exists but insufficient to decide.
  • DZ — Contradicted: significant discrepancy under declared criteria.
  • ∞/∞ — Not Testable: absence of comparable, defined, or accessible data.
